Callulose Based Passive Daytime Radiative Cooling (PDRC) Material

PDRC in Shelters - Heat Transfer Simulations


Project: Establish the shelter shape that would maintain the lowest temperature the longest with the PDRC coating. ​
​
Approach: Five various shapes were identified (two are shown above) for further PDRC material application to passively cool down the shelter. PDRC material achieves cooling through refelcting most of the visible light and emitting most of the infrared light that it absorbed. The shape of the shelter is important because of the volume to area ratio. A study was conducted using Ansys Fluent to determine which shape performes best where all shapes had the same volume but varying area. Discrete Ordinates model was used to simulate gray-body radiation across three different spectra, both transient and steady-state simulations were conducted.

Outcome: Six-sided shape performed best with steady-state value of volume-average temperature of 318.25K which is a few degrees cooler than other shapes.

Passive Daytime Radiative Cooling (PDRC) in shelters

Radiative and thermal property analysis


Project: Based on research, a scaled shelter prorotype with PDRC coating was built. The goal was to test its performace against the prototype with no PDRC coating using simulated solar radiation. ​
​
Approach: The model was created using SolidWorks with the structure materials thermal and radiative properties analyzed using FT/IR Spectrometers and Hot Disk Instruments. The walls of the protoype were insualted throughout all of the testing, while the roof was tested in four configurations: 1)PDRC 2)Insulation 3)PDRC & Insulation 4)Neither. Four thermocouples tracked temperatures of the prototype and ambience until they achieved steady-state.

Outcome: Cellulose based PDRC Material (referred as paper in the image) achieved the same cooling performance as an inch-thick heat insulation, proving that this technology can be useful if scaled up.

Engine Block Deformation Study

Engine Block - Deformation simulations


Project: Analyse whether the engine block in the internal combustion engine would fail under the gas pressure exerted onto it. ​
​
Approach: The engine block was modeled in SolidWorks and the model was then exported to Ansys Fluent for deformation and stress analysis. For more accurate results, a mesh study was conducted.

Outcome: Maximum Von Mises stress did not exceed the yield stress.

Seagrass in Tidal Flow Study

Seagrass in Tidal Flow - Fluid velocity and vorticity analysis


Project: Find out the best type of seaweed to artificially build up the shoreline for erosion protection. ​
​
Approach: ANSYS Fluent was used to perform transient simulations of a turbulent ocean shore flow over simple formations of three types of seagrass: H. Ovalis, Z. Capricorni and P. Australis. After creating the simplified 3D models using SolidWorks and importing them into ANSYS, various degree of accuracy in meshing were tested. The simulation used a k-epsilon model.

Outcome: Drag coefficient, drag force, and vorticity data for each run were collected and analysed in MATLAB. As a result, P. Australis species were chosen as the preferred selection due to them experience the least drag force.
